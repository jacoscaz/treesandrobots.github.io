  • All weight lifting movements must be performed with either sandbags or kettlebells.
  • Each set should be a hard set, taken to 0 RIR (Reps In Reserve). Select movements, weights, variations and intensity that allow for 5 - 20 reps. Perform controlled, deliberate movements, maximizing time under tension and minimizing inertia. Pause at both the bottom and top positions. Faster concentric, slower eccentric.
  • Perform all movements to the fullest extent of their range, i.e. full ROM, stopping just short of full joint lock and/or any significant decrease in muscle tension, particularly when in the resting position. A full ROM pull-up is not the same thing as a dead hang pull-up.
  • Practice and warm up to each movement. Keep track of your center of gravity as it moves across the body. Pay attention to where it lands within your feet and hands. Watch out for imbalances. Focus on posture, shoulders and shoulder blades. Always engage the core. End each workout with a few stretches and mobility exercises.
  • Technique failure is still failure.
  • Prefer variations that allow for joint rotation throughout the movement, such as variations performed on gymnastic rings. If rotation is not an option, prefer neutral grips. Beware of using rings too soon!
  • Prefer more variations that better translate to routine movement patterns.
  • Every now and then replace one daily session with 6 sets of Clean & Press.

Hip and shoulder work

Practice regularly, on their own and/or as a part of warming up, particularly if you spend a lot of time using computers.

  • Handstand and manna (L-sit, V-sit, …)
  • Active (hollow) and passive hanging
  • Grok squats (w/ arm reach)
  • Back bridge
  • Skin-the-cat
  • Shoulder raise
  • Halo
  • Bottoms-up

Floor culture

  • Easy pose (Sukhasana)
  • 90/90 pose
  • Kneeling / toe sit
  • Straddle
  • Sphinx
  • On the side

Weekly schedule

  • 6 strength workouts (mon - sat)
  • 1 sprinting or HIIT session
  • 2 to 5 hr of low-intensity exercise (walk, play, trek, bike, skate, rucking, …)

Core movements

  • Crunch (side, tabletop, toe reach, cross toe reach, knee-to-elbow)

  • Plank (front, side, side with rotations, reverse plank)
  • Leg raise (hanging, floor)
  • Core hinging (ab rollout, inch worm)
  • Anti-rotational (side-to-side plank pull, side-to-side plank push)
  • Back Bridge
  • Back extension (superman, 4-point alt. kneel. leg & arm extensions, fitball)
